Last Release

We are pleased to present our latest release: Bingo Online. This is a revamped version of the classic bingo game, adapted for the digital world, allowing each player to enjoy a personalized experience on their own device. With this version, we have integrated a series of improvements to ensure that each game is dynamic, accessible, and highly entertaining. Now, it is no longer necessary to share a single physical board: each player can participate with their own computer. This modality ensures that the game is more comfortable, interactive, and adaptable to different needs, allowing friends and family to gather and enjoy together regardless of the distance.



How to Play

It has a simple operation, starting with a tab to enter your name. Once entered, a card with 3 lines and 10 columns will be generated, with only the first 9 columns containing random numbers (numbers from 1 to 90) and some random empty spaces. It is designed so that each row has 5 numbers. You can mark the numbers by clicking on them. Line: Once you have a line, you can press the button on the left that says "Line" and it will verify if the line is correct or not. If it is not correct, you will be penalized by blocking the "Line" button for 5 seconds. If the line is correct, a tab will open with a question and several answers. If you fail, it will show you the correct answer in green and you will not have the line, so another player can make it. While you answer the question, the game stops, so you don't have to answer quickly.

Host

The device assigned as Host plays a fundamental role in the development of the game. On the Host's screen, a virtual board will appear with 100 balls numbered from 1 to 100, representing the complete set of possible outcomes. As the host draws the balls, these numbers will be automatically crossed out, making it easy to track the balls that have already been drawn and those that are yet to appear. Additionally, the Host has a "Next Ball" button, which allows controlled progression in each round, ensuring that players have time to locate and mark the numbers on their boards before continuing. This tool provides complete control over the pace of the game, allowing for a fun and uninterrupted experience.


User

For the player, the device acts as their personal bingo board. At the start, the user will be asked to choose a unique name to identify themselves within the game (this is important to differentiate from other players in the same game). Once the name is entered, the player will see their own bingo card, with a random combination of numbers that they must mark as the Host announces the drawn balls. Players can use buttons to mark or unmark the selected numbers on their card, making it easy to keep track of the plays. When the player's card is complete with a line, column, or special pattern, they can press the "Bingo!" button to announce their victory. In case of an error, nothing happens: the game is designed to be flexible, and you can continue playing without penalty. Additionally, the verification system prevents an incorrect bingo from being announced, maintaining the integrity of the game for all participants.
